history

Nike is a global sports footwear and apparel company that was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman. The company officially changed its name to Nike in 1971, named after the Greek goddess of victory.

In the beginning, Nike focused on producing high-quality running shoes, and it quickly gained a reputation as a leader in the industry. Over the years, the company has expanded its product line to include shoes for a wide variety of sports and activities, as well as casual and lifestyle footwear.

Nike is known for its innovative designs and use of advanced technologies in its shoes, as well as its partnerships with top athletes and sports teams. Today, Nike is one of the most recognized and respected brands in the world, with a strong presence in the global sports market.
